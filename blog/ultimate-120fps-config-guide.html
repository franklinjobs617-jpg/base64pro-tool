Why Are Config Files Necessary?

Configuration files are required because mobile game developers often restrict higher FPS options based on detected device models, even if the hardware is technically capable of running at higher rates. By utilizing an external config file, you are essentially providing the game with optimized settings to bypass these artificial limits, achieving better performance, reduced lag, and improved responsiveness.

Manual Risk vs. Automated Safety

The journey to 120 FPS often involves dealing with encoded data strings—a necessity for content creators to protect their shared files. However, manually handling Base64 code can be problematic:

  • "Shine Ads" Friction: Many sources force users through complex download funnels filled with invasive ads and redirects, wasting time and causing frustration.

This is where automation becomes vital. By using specialized tools, you ensure accuracy, speed, and safety. Whether you are dealing with a direct download or a Base64 string, our process simplifies the journey.

Security and Safety: Know the Risks

While config files significantly improve performance (reducing overheating, lag, and joystick issues), you are modifying game files. This is generally against the game's Terms of Service (ToS).

Use at Your Own Risk: Although many players use FPS configs without issues, there is always a potential risk of an account ban. We provide the tools and guides, but the decision to use third-party config files is solely yours.

Quick Troubleshooting

  • Still stuck at 60 FPS? Ensure you have cleared the game cache and that your device's display refresh rate is set to 90Hz or 120Hz in your system settings.
  • Decoding Issues? Our dedicated converter tool is optimized for large data blocks. If you encounter an error, ensure the entire Base64 string was copied correctly.
